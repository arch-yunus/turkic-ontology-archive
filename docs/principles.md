# Philosophical Principles of the Turkic Ontology

The Turkic Ontology is a holistic system that integrates the divine, the natural, and the social into a single cohesive reality.

## 1. The Tripartite Universe (Üç Dünyalı Evren)
Ancient Turkic cosmology divides existence into three interconnected realms:

- **The Upper World (Üst Dünya):** The realm of Gök Tengri and celestial spirits. It represents the source of life, wisdom, and moral law.
- **The Middle World (Orta Dünya):** The realm of humans, nature (Yer-Su), and animals. It is the stage where the divine mandate (Kut) is enacted.
- **The Lower World (Alt Dünya):** The realm of ancestors, spirits of the past, and Erlik. It is not necessarily "evil" in the Western sense but represents the hidden, darker, and transitional aspects of existence.

## 2. Cosmic Balance (Denge ve Uyum)
Everything in the universe is governed by the principle of balance. Humans are not rulers of nature but participants in it. Any disruption to the natural order (e.g., polluting water, unjust rule) causes a "shaking" of the cosmic balance, leading to catastrophe.

## 3. The Mandate of Kut
Power is not inherited as a property but granted as a responsibility. 
- **Divine Origin:** Kut comes from Tengri.
- **Moral Condition:** It remains with the ruler only as long as they are "bilig" (wise) and "alp" (courageous) and uphold the "Töre" (justice).
- **Service to the People:** The primary role of the Khagan is to ensure the people are fed, safe, and united.

## 4. Continuity of Töre
The "Töre" (Customary Law) is seen as more permanent than any state or ruler. A Turkic proverb says: *"The state might crumble, but the Töre remains."* It represents the ontological backbone of society.
